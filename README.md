NovaRetail+ Customer Behavior Correlation Analysis

Project Overview

NovaRetail+ is a large e-commerce platform operating in Latin America with millions of users.

This exploratory data analysis project focuses on identifying which customer behavior factors are most strongly associated with annual revenue generated by users.

The project combines statistical analysis, correlation methods, and business-oriented interpretation to better understand user behavior patterns and potential business insights.

⸻

Business Question

Which customer behavior factors are most strongly associated with annual revenue generated?

⸻

Project Workflow

1. Initial Data Exploration

* Verified missing values
* Reviewed data types
* Identified numerical, binary, and categorical variables
* Converted edad from float64 to int64

⸻

2. Statistical Analysis

Performed descriptive statistics using .describe() to analyze:

* central tendency
* dispersion
* variability
* possible skewness

Main observations:

* ingreso_anual showed signs of right skewness
* nivel_ingreso presented high economic variability

⸻

3. Binary Variable Analysis

Validated binary variables (0/1) and identified category imbalance in:

* miembro_premium
* abandono

⸻

4. Categorical Variable Analysis

Analyzed:

* device type distribution
* regional distribution

Key findings:

* 65% of users access the platform through mobile devices
* North region represented the highest concentration of users (~29%)

⸻

Correlation Analysis

Correlation Heatmap Findings

Key relationships identified:

* Strong collinearity between compras_mes and ingreso_anual (0.97)
* Moderate positive correlation between gasto_publicidad_dirigida and visitas_mes (0.58)
* Moderate relationship between visitas_mes and compras_mes (0.35)
* Weak negative correlation between miembro_premium and abandono (-0.12)

⸻

Correlation Methods Used

Pearson Correlation

Used for:

* gasto_publicidad_dirigida vs visitas_mes

Result:

* Positive moderate correlation (0.58)

Interpretation:
Higher advertising investment tends to be associated with increased monthly visits.

⸻

Spearman Correlation

Used for:

* visitas_mes vs compras_mes

Result:

* Positive moderate-low correlation (0.33)

Interpretation:
Users with higher platform activity tend to perform more purchases.

⸻

Point-Biserial Correlation

Used for:

* miembro_premium vs ingreso_anual

Result:

* Weak positive correlation (0.093)

Interpretation:
Premium users tend to generate slightly higher annual revenue, although the relationship is weak.

⸻

Main Business Insights

Insight 1

Advertising investment appears to be positively associated with platform traffic.

Potential business implication:

* optimizing targeted campaigns could help increase user engagement and visits.

⸻

Insight 2

Users with higher interaction frequency tend to perform more purchases.

Potential business implication:

* increasing user engagement may create more purchase opportunities.

⸻

Limitations

* Correlation does not imply causation
* Some relationships may be influenced by external variables not included in the dataset
* Certain variables present category imbalance
* Further multivariable analysis is required for stronger conclusions
